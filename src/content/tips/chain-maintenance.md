You don't need a workshop to look after a chain. A centre stand (or a mate to hold the
bike upright and roll it) gets you 90% of the way there.

## The routine

1. **Clean** while warm-ish after a ride — grime comes off easier. Kerosene or dedicated
   chain cleaner on a brush, then wipe with a rag.
2. **Inspect** for tight spots: rotate the wheel slowly and watch for links that bind.
3. **Lube** the *inside* of the lower run so centrifugal force carries it outward. Let it
   sit 10 minutes before riding so it doesn't fling off.

## Slack check

Measure at the tightest point in the chain's rotation, not wherever you happen to stop.
Set it to the manual's spec — too tight is far worse for bearings than slightly loose.
